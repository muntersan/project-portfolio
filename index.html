Get to know me

I wrote my first code when I was 12 years old, it was a blog design for my friend. This was back when blogging was the shit, and everyone wanted new themes every month and, obviously, a snowflake effect for Christmas. I can’t promise it looked good, but I did learn basic HTML and CSS. My friends were impressed and I had a lot of fun doing it. Sadly I forgot about this hobby for years after that, but along came the pandemic and in that stay-home bubble I re-discovered code. I spent the last couple of years learning it properly and took a course including front- and back-end development and visual design. I am a front-end developer at heart, but if you need a website designed, or published, I can do that too.

What do I do?

I enjoy creating responsive websites, I start from desktop and work down to smaller screen sizes so the page will also look flawless on a smartphone. Responsiveness is my favorite part of the development process, and I love to spend a lot of time on that. This year I am also looking forward to learning all there is to know about inclusive web design. Accessibility is often overlooked everywhere in society, including the internet.
